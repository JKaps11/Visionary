# Visionary — Purpose

## The problem

The moment your mind is racing — in bed, on a walk, mid-shower — existing note apps get in the way. They make you unlock, choose a notebook, pick a tag, write a title. The idea is gone before you've filed it. Existing tools are built for organizing, not capturing.

## The user's emotional state

Not drowsy. **Excited.** The user is electric, lit up, has something to say. They opened the app *because of the thought*. The thought is the reason. The app should match that energy and get out of the way, not dampen it.

## What the app does

Visionary is a pocket notebook for racing minds. You open it, the cursor is already blinking on a blank page, the keyboard is already up. You type the thought. You lock the phone. Done. When you want to look back, you swipe up to flip the page like a real notebook, and your past thoughts are underneath.

The app has two modes — capture and retrieval — and they share a database but never share a screen. The user is in exactly one mode at a time.

- **Capture** is the front door. Every open lands here. Blank page, cursor ready, keyboard up. Typing is primary. Voice is available via a single mic button for the longer "talk it out" thoughts.
- **Retrieval** is one gesture away. Swipe up to flip back through the notebook. Chronological, calm, browsable.

A separate web app exists for sit-down sessions: an AI-generated mind map that shows the lineage and connections between thoughts, and AI idea evaluation. The mobile app is the notebook; the web app is where you wander, analyze, and build on what you've collected.

## The CTA

**Put more great ideas into the world.**

Capture is the mechanism. Output is the point. The app exists to remove every obstacle between having a thought and saving it, so that more of those thoughts survive long enough to become something.

## What the app is not

- Not an all-in-one notes app
- Not a to-do list
- Not a journaling app
- Not an organizational system with folders, tags, or projects
- Not a place to edit and polish — it's a place to capture
- Not a writing tool — it's a thinking tool
